Privacy Policy

Vivian Ho, MD

Your Privacy Rights

At Ho Psychiatry, we are committed to protecting your privacy and maintaining the confidentiality of your personal health information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and otherwise handle your information.

HIPAA Compliance

Ho Psychiatry complies with all applicable federal and state privacy laws, including the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). We maintain physical, electronic, and procedural safeguards to protect your health information from unauthorized access, use, and disclosure.

Information We Collect

We collect health information and personal information necessary to provide you with psychiatric care and services. This includes:

  • Your name, contact information, and insurance details
  • Medical and mental health history
  • Information about your current symptoms and treatment
  • Emergency contact information

How We Use Your Information

Your information is used to:

  • Provide psychiatric evaluation and treatment
  • Coordinate care with other healthcare providers (with your consent)
  • Process insurance claims and billing
  • Maintain medical records
  • Comply with legal and regulatory requirements
  • Improve our services

Disclosure of Information

Ho Psychiatry will not disclose your health information without your written consent, except as required or permitted by law. Permitted disclosures include:

  • To healthcare providers involved in your treatment
  • To your insurance company for billing and authorization
  • When required by court order or legal process
  • To prevent serious harm to you or others
  • For public health activities or law enforcement purposes as required by law

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access your medical records
  • Request corrections to your health information
  • Request an accounting of disclosures made about you
  • Request restrictions on the use and disclosure of your health information
  • Receive communications about your health in a confidential manner
  • File a complaint with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services if you believe your privacy rights have been violated

Online Communication

Please note that email and other online communication methods are not completely secure. While we take precautions to protect your information, you acknowledge that there are inherent risks in sending sensitive health information via email or other unencrypted methods. Any sensitive information you share through unsecured channels is done at your own discretion.

Changes to This Policy

Ho Psychiatry may update this Privacy Policy at any time. We will notify you of any material changes by posting the updated policy on this website.
