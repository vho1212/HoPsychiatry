About Me

I’m a Stanford-trained psychiatrist and psychotherapist offering care for California and Wisconsin patients.

My approach to psychiatry is grounded in curiosity, collaboration, and deep respect for each person's lived experience. I believe that understanding why you're struggling is just as important as helping you feel better, and I see our work as a partnership. I combine therapy with thoughtful medication management when it's helpful, and I also offer medication management as a standalone service for people who already have a therapist or those seeking only medications.

I work with adults from all walks of life and have a special commitment to supporting BIPOC and LGBTQ+ communities. I specialize in include women's mental health, trauma, relationships, and life transitions.

I’m deeply interested in the connection between mind and body, and I believe that mental health isn't something that happens only "in your head." Along with additional training in trauma therapy, I've maintained a vipassana (insight) meditation practice for more than 8+ years, including nearly a year spent on silent retreats. I also offer couples and group therapy because I believe healing often happens in relationship.

Before opening my private practice, I co-founded Stanford's first Ketamine-Assisted Psychotherapy Clinic, served as a therapist on an MDMA-assisted therapy trial for PTSD, and helped create and teach Stanford's course Psychedelics and Social Justice. While I don't currently offer psychedelic-assisted therapy, these experiences continue to shape my thoughtful, integrative approach to care.

My Approach

As both a psychiatrist and therapist, I believe healing is about more than feeling less anxious, less depressed, or less overwhelmed. It's about reconnecting with yourself- your mind, your body, your relationships, and the parts of you that may have been overlooked for a long time. I value curiosity over judgment, honesty with compassion, and working at a pace that feels both safe and productive. Whether we're focusing on therapy, medication, or both, my goal is to help you build a life that feels more connected and meaningful.
