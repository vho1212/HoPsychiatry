About Me

I’m a Stanford-trained psychiatrist and psychotherapist offering care for California and Wisconsin patients.

A calm, thoughtful approach to mental well-being.

My approach to psychiatry is rooted in curiosity, collaboration, and deep respect for each person's lived experience. My primary approach combines psychodynamic psychotherapy with thoughtful, evidence-based medication management when appropriate. I also provide medication management as a standalone service for those already engaged in therapy or seeking pharmacologic treatment.

I work with adults from all walks of life and have a special passion for supporting BIPOC and LGBTQ+ communities. My clinical interests include women's mental health across the lifespan, trauma, relationships, life transitions. I’ve sought out dedicated training in trauma work, and I have years of experience and months of dedicated silent retreat practice in vipassana meditation. I also provide couples and group psychotherapy, believing that healing often happens not only within ourselves but through our relationships with others.

Before entering private practice, I co-founded Stanford's first Ketamine-Assisted Psychotherapy Clinic. While I do not currently offer psychedelic-assisted psychotherapy, that experience continues to inform my thoughtful, integrative approach to care.

My Approach

As both a psychiatrist and therapist, I believe the best care looks beyond symptom relief. I value curiosity over judgment, honesty with compassion, and working at a pace that feels both safe and productive. Whether we're focusing on therapy, medication, or both, my goal is to help you develop lasting insight and practical tools that support a more fulfilling life.
